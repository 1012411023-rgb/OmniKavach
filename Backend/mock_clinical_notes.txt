PATIENT A — ICU Nursing Shift Note (Sepsis Presentation)
================================================================================
Patient ID: P-101 | Date: Current Shift | Time: 04:00

SHIFT ASSESSMENT:
Patient presents with concerning clinical picture requiring close monitoring. Noted
to be lethargic with decreased responsiveness to verbal stimuli. Skin appears clammy
and cool to touch, suggesting peripheral perfusion compromise. Febrile to 38.9°C
with rigors. Patient appears confused and disoriented to time and place.

VITAL SIGNS:
- Heart Rate: 118 bpm (tachycardic)
- Blood Pressure: 88/52 mmHg (hypotensive)
- Respiratory Rate: 24/min (elevated)
- Temperature: 38.9°C (febrile)
- SpO2: 91% on room air

PHYSICAL FINDINGS:
- General: Febrile, clammy skin, diaphoretic, poor turgor
- Neurological: Confused, GCS 12, not oriented to time/place
- Respiratory: Tachypneic, shallow breathing pattern
- Cardiovascular: Tachycardic, weak peripheral pulses, capillary refill >3s

CLINICAL CONCERNS:
1. Sepsis likely — fever + tachycardia + hypotension + confusion
2. Tissue hypoperfusion — clammy skin + altered mental status + delayed cap refill
3. Respiratory compromise — tachypnea + borderline hypoxia
4. Possible organ dysfunction — monitor renal output and lactate

RECOMMENDATIONS:
- STAT lactate, blood cultures x2, CBC, CMP, procalcitonin
- Initiate Surviving Sepsis Campaign Hour-1 bundle
- 30 mL/kg crystalloid bolus
- Broad-spectrum antibiotics within 1 hour
- Continuous cardiac monitoring, vitals every 15 min

NURSING ACTIONS:
- Two large-bore peripheral IVs placed
- Fluid bolus 1L NS initiated
- Blood cultures drawn prior to antibiotics
- Foley catheter placed — urine output monitoring initiated
- Continuous pulse oximetry and telemetry applied

- Cardiovascular: Regular rate, no murmur; radiating pain to left arm and jaw
- Respiratory: Clear bilaterally, no crackles
- Neurological: Alert and oriented x4, GCS 15

CLINICAL CONCERNS:
1. Acute Coronary Syndrome — chest pressure + radiation + diaphoresis
2. Rule out STEMI — obtain 12-lead ECG immediately
3. Hemodynamic stress — hypertension and mild tachycardia
4. Risk stratification required — troponin, CK-MB

RECOMMENDATIONS:
- STAT 12-lead ECG, serial troponins q3h
- Aspirin 325mg chewed, sublingual nitroglycerin PRN

- Cardiology consult, prepare for potential cath lab activation
- Continuous telemetry, vitals every 10 min

- IV access x2 established

- NTG 0.4mg SL x1 administered — partial relief reported
