# Surviving Sepsis Campaign Hour-1 Bundle

## Overview
The Surviving Sepsis Campaign Hour-1 Bundle provides evidence-based recommendations for the early management of sepsis and septic shock. These interventions should be initiated within the first hour of recognition.

## Core Components

### 1. Measure Lactate Level
- **Action**: Obtain blood lactate measurement
- **Rationale**: Elevated lactate (>2 mmol/L) indicates tissue hypoperfusion
- **Follow-up**: Re-measure lactate if initial level is elevated
- **Target**: Lactate clearance ≥10% per hour

### 2. Obtain Blood Cultures
- **Action**: Draw blood cultures before antibiotic administration
- **Quantity**: At least two sets from different sites
- **Timing**: Within 1 hour of presentation
- **Rationale**: Identify causative pathogen for targeted therapy

### 3. Administer Broad-Spectrum Antibiotics
- **Action**: Initiate empiric broad-spectrum antibiotics
- **Timing**: Within 1 hour of recognition
- **Selection**: Cover likely pathogens (Gram-positive, Gram-negative, anaerobes)
- **Adjustment**: De-escalate based on culture results and clinical response
- **Duration**: Typically 7-10 days if source controlled

### 4. Begin Fluid Resuscitation
- **Action**: Administer crystalloid fluid bolus
- **Initial Dose**: 30 mL/kg (ideal body weight)
- **Type**: Balanced crystalloids preferred (e.g., Lactated Ringer's, Plasma-Lyte)
- **Indications**: Hypotension (MAP <65 mmHg) or lactate ≥4 mmol/L
- **Reassessment**: Continuously evaluate for fluid responsiveness

## Additional Considerations

### Vasopressor Support
- **Indication**: Persistent hypotension despite fluid resuscitation
- **Target**: MAP ≥65 mmHg
- **First-line**: Norepinephrine
- **Alternatives**: Vasopressin, epinephrine, dopamine (limited indications)

### Source Control
- **Timeline**: Within 12 hours of recognition
- **Actions**: Drain infected collections, debride infected tissue, remove devices
- **Imaging**: As needed for source identification

### Monitoring Parameters
- **Hemodynamics**: Blood pressure, heart rate, MAP, urine output
- **Perfusion**: Lactate levels, capillary refill, skin mottling
- **Organ Function**: Renal function, mental status, respiratory status

## Quality Metrics
- Time to lactate measurement
- Time to blood culture draw
- Time to antibiotic administration
- Time to fluid resuscitation initiation
- 30-day mortality
- Length of ICU stay

## Clinical Pearls
- **Time is tissue**: Every hour delay in appropriate therapy increases mortality
- **Bundle compliance**: Associated with 25% relative risk reduction in mortality
- **Reassessment key**: Continuously re-evaluate response to interventions
- **Source control**: Essential for definitive management
