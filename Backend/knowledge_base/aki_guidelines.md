# Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) — Clinical Guidelines

## Definition & Staging (KDIGO 2023)

Acute Kidney Injury is defined by any of the following:
- Increase in serum creatinine ≥ 0.3 mg/dL within 48 hours
- Increase in serum creatinine ≥ 1.5× baseline within 7 days
- Urine output < 0.5 mL/kg/hr for 6 hours

### KDIGO Staging

| Stage | Serum Creatinine Criteria | Urine Output Criteria |
|-------|---------------------------|----------------------|
| 1 | 1.5–1.9× baseline OR ≥ 0.3 mg/dL increase | < 0.5 mL/kg/hr for 6–12h |
| 2 | 2.0–2.9× baseline | < 0.5 mL/kg/hr for ≥ 12h |
| 3 | 3.0× baseline OR ≥ 4.0 mg/dL OR initiation of RRT | < 0.3 mL/kg/hr for ≥ 24h OR anuria ≥ 12h |

## ICU Management Protocol

### Immediate Actions
1. **Volume assessment** — assess fluid status (JVP, lung auscultation, peripheral edema)
2. **Discontinue nephrotoxins** — NSAIDs, aminoglycosides, IV contrast, ACE-inhibitors (if hypotensive)
3. **Optimize hemodynamics** — target MAP ≥ 65 mmHg with fluids ± vasopressors
4. **Urinary catheter** — strict I/O monitoring, hourly urine output measurement

### Laboratory Monitoring
- Serum creatinine every 12h during acute phase
- Electrolytes (K⁺, Na⁺, Ca²⁺, PO₄³⁻) every 6–12h
- Blood gas for acid-base status
- Urinalysis with microscopy and urine electrolytes

### Indications for Renal Replacement Therapy (RRT / CRRT)
Urgent RRT indications include:
- Refractory hyperkalemia (K⁺ > 6.5 mEq/L despite medical management)
- Severe metabolic acidosis (pH < 7.1) unresponsive to bicarbonate
- Refractory fluid overload with pulmonary edema
- Uremic complications (encephalopathy, pericarditis, bleeding)
- BUN > 80–100 mg/dL with symptoms

### Sepsis-Associated AKI
- AKI occurs in 40–50% of septic shock patients
- Treat underlying sepsis aggressively per Surviving Sepsis Campaign guidelines
- Avoid excessive fluid resuscitation after initial resuscitation phase
- Early nephrology consultation recommended for Stage 2+ AKI in sepsis
