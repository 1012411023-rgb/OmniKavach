# Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome (ARDS) — Clinical Guidelines

## Definition (Berlin Criteria 2012)

ARDS is an acute, diffuse, inflammatory lung injury leading to:
- **Timing**: Onset within 1 week of known clinical insult or new/worsening respiratory symptoms
- **Imaging**: Bilateral opacities on chest X-ray not fully explained by effusions, atelectasis, or nodules
- **Origin**: Respiratory failure not fully explained by cardiac failure or fluid overload
- **Oxygenation impairment** (with PEEP ≥ 5 cm H₂O):

### Severity Classification

| Severity | PaO₂/FiO₂ Ratio | Mortality |
|----------|------------------|-----------|
| Mild | 200–300 mmHg | ~27% |
| Moderate | 100–200 mmHg | ~32% |
| Severe | < 100 mmHg | ~45% |

## ICU Management Protocol

### Ventilator Strategy (ARDSNet Protocol)
1. **Low tidal volume ventilation** — Target Vt 6 mL/kg ideal body weight (IBW)
2. **Plateau pressure** — Maintain Pplat ≤ 30 cm H₂O
3. **PEEP titration** — Use ARDSNet PEEP/FiO₂ table; higher PEEP for severe ARDS
4. **Driving pressure** — Target ΔP ≤ 15 cm H₂O (Pplat − PEEP)
5. **Permissive hypercapnia** — Accept PaCO₂ up to 60 mmHg if pH > 7.20

### Rescue Therapies (Severe ARDS — P/F < 150)
- **Prone positioning** — ≥ 16 hours/day for P/F < 150 (PROSEVA trial)
- **Neuromuscular blockade** — Consider cisatracurium for first 48h if severe dyssynchrony
- **Inhaled pulmonary vasodilators** — iNO or epoprostenol for refractory hypoxemia
- **ECMO referral** — Consider if P/F < 80 despite optimal ventilator management and prone positioning

### Fluid Management
- **Conservative fluid strategy** (FACTT trial) once hemodynamically stable
- Target CVP < 4 mmHg or PAOP < 8 mmHg if available
- Diuretics to achieve even-to-negative fluid balance after initial resuscitation

### Adjunctive Measures
- **Sedation**: Light sedation protocol (PADIS guidelines), daily interruption trials
- **DVT prophylaxis**: LMWH unless contraindicated
- **Stress ulcer prophylaxis**: PPI or H2-blocker
- **Nutrition**: Early enteral nutrition within 24–48h, trophic feeds initially
- **Glucose control**: Target 140–180 mg/dL

## Sepsis-Associated ARDS
- ARDS occurs in ~25% of patients with septic shock
- Treat underlying infection aggressively — antibiotics remain the primary intervention
- Lung-protective ventilation is mandatory regardless of etiology
- Monitor for multi-organ dysfunction — concurrent AKI is common
